# Contributor Code of Conduct

Welcome to the ArchBench community! This project is a space for developers, system architects, and AI builders to collaborate, experiment, and build better systems. We value curiosity, craftsmanship, technical rigor, respectful disagreement, and the responsible development of AI and developer tools.

To ensure our community remains collaborative, productive, and welcoming to everyone, we ask all participants to adhere to this Code of Conduct.

---

## 1. Expected Behavior

We encourage participants to foster a healthy environment by:
* **Respecting Everyone:** Welcome and support contributors of all backgrounds, identity details, and experience levels.
* **Providing Constructive Feedback:** Focus critiques on ideas, architectures, and code quality, rather than individuals. Focus on solutions and collaborative problem-solving.
* **Encouraging Learning:** Help newcomers understand the codebase, tools, and workflows. Sharing knowledge is central to our mission.
* **Assuming Positive Intent:** Start from the assumption that other contributors want to improve the project. If disagreement arises, seek clarification in good faith.
* **Striving for Excellence & Craftsmanship:** Build tools with care, document decisions clearly, and consider the long-term impact on the development ecosystem.
* **Practicing Transparency:** Keep discussions and development decisions open and public whenever possible.

---

## 2. Unacceptable Behavior

To protect our community, the following behaviors are strictly prohibited:
* **Harassment and Discrimination:** Hate speech, discriminatory jokes, intimidation, bullying, stalking, or exclusionary language targeting individuals or groups based on race, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, or religion.
* **Personal Attacks:** Insults, threats of violence, or derogatory remarks directed at other contributors.
* **Disruptive Behavior:** Deliberate trolling, bad-faith arguments, inflammatory comments, spamming, and intentional disruption of conversations or project discussions.
* **Privacy Violations:** Publishing private, confidential, or sensitive personal information (such as email addresses, physical addresses, or phone numbers) without explicit, documented permission.
* **Exclusionary Conduct:** Behavior that systematically makes others feel unwelcome or prevents active, constructive contribution to the project.

---

## 3. Scope and Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for keeping discussions healthy, clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior, and taking fair and appropriate corrective action.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to:
* Edit, redact, or remove comments, commits, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that do not align this Code of Conduct.
* Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

---

## 4. Reporting

If you observe or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it immediately:
* **Open an Issue or Discussion:** For public concerns, please open a GitHub Issue or join a thread in GitHub Discussions.
* **Direct Contact:** If the issue is sensitive or requires private resolution, please reach out directly to the project maintainers.

We are committed to addressing all reports promptly, confidentially, and with fairness.
